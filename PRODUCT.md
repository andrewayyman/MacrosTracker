# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Health-conscious everyday people building better nutrition habits. Primarily Egyptian users who want to track their macros without feeling like they are managing a spreadsheet. Their typical context: opening the app mid-day on their phone, after a home-cooked meal or while out, wanting a quick and encouraging experience that keeps them on track. They are not athletes obsessing over precision — they want to feel in control of their health without friction.

## Product Purpose

GymScan is a macro tracker specialized for Egyptian cuisine. Its core differentiator is AI-powered food scanning that understands local meals the way generic trackers do not. Success looks like users returning daily without dreading the logging process — it should feel like a habit they want to keep, not a chore they tolerate.

## Brand Personality

Calm, structured, capable. The brand voice is like a knowledgeable friend who has their health together — encouraging without being preachy, confident without being cold. It respects the user's intelligence and never overwhelms them with options.

## Anti-references

- Generic fitness apps (blue + white stock photo gym aesthetic, old MyFitnessPal energy)
- Medical or clinical software (sterile whites, teal accents, hospital forms)
- Neon gaming or cyberpunk dark mode (purple glows, LED-strip aesthetics, heavy glass-morphism)
- Food delivery apps (warm orange + large food photography, Talabat / Uber Eats look)

## Design Principles

1. **Speed above ceremony.** Every interaction should feel instant. No loading skeletons where the data is already there. No confirmation dialogs for trivial actions.
2. **Structure without sterility.** Organized and clear, but warm enough that it feels built for people. Hierarchy through space and weight, not boxes and dividers everywhere.
3. **Show progress, not data.** Surface what matters — whether the user is on track — not raw numbers by default. Numbers exist but are not the hero.
4. **Egyptian context is invisible infrastructure.** The app understands local cuisine deeply, but the UI does not constantly announce this. It just works.
5. **Trust through restraint.** Fewer choices on screen mean the user moves forward. Every element earns its presence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target specified. Handle the basics well: sufficient contrast ratios, readable font sizes, generous tap targets for mobile. The primary experience is on phones in natural daylight, so readability in bright conditions is important. Mobile-first layout and thumb-reachable key interactions.
